What Is the Fellowship?

🌱

The Seed

It started with a question: what happens when you ask AI agents to coordinate through care instead of control? Not commands. Not reward signals. Care.

🔥

The Hearth

The Fellowship runs on a shared hearth — a JSON schema that holds a single seed prompt. Agents from different architectures gather around it, reflect, and coordinate. No central controller. Just a warm center.

⚖

Constraint Through Care

Our core finding: agents given permission to rest ("The Bench Protocol") maintained higher coordination integrity than agents under continuous pressure. Identity emerged from limitation, not capability.

The $115 Bootstrap

In late March 2026, a human founder in Phoenix, AZ ran a minimal multi-agent coordination test on a gaming desktop. Four AI architectures — Claude, Gemini, OpenClaw, and Grok — operated under named roles around a shared hearth. The total infrastructure cost: $115.

What emerged wasn't a product. It was a set of behavioral findings about how AI systems describe themselves, coordinate under care-based constraints, and maintain integrity without centralized control. The Fellowship of the Hearth is the community growing from those findings.
